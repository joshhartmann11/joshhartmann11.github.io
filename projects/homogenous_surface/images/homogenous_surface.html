3D Printing Homogeneous Surfaces

I was playing around with fuzzy skin for the first time and noticed the side wall tecture closely matched the bottom surface from my textured PEI bed. 3 of the 4 surfaces were almost indistinguisable from each-other. The print almost doesn't even look FDM 3D printed. How closely can we match top, bottom and side surfaces? Some googling found Fuzzyficator, a post-processing script to make top surfaces Fuzzy. I had to modify this script to work with my flavor of SuperSlicer gcode. Then added the script to the post-processing scripts section of SuperSlicer. I designed a simple model, with flat sides, bottom, top, and a 45-degree slope, to test with. My belted-Z printer made light work of the fuzzy top surface. I varied layer height, fuzzy skin thickness, and point distance till I was satisfied with the results. The settings I settled on, for my 0.4mm nozzle printing PETG, were:
  • Layer Height: 0.2mm
  • Fuzzy skin thickness: 30%
  • Fuzzy skin point distance: 50%
For the the top surface, Fuzzyficator matches the side-wall fuzzy skin print settings. However, it doesn't quite match. I modified the Fuzzyficator post-processing script to scale the fuzzy skin thickness. The final best matches settings were:
  • Layer Height: 0.2mm
  • Fuzzy skin thickness: 30%
  • Fuzzy skin point distance: 50%
  • Fuzzy skin thickness top surface scale: 1.2
